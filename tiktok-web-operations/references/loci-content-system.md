# Loci TikTok Content System

Treat this as a provisional starting point until the live account, audience region, language, and analytics are audited.

## Account strategy

Do not start as a generic app, founder, or AIGC-tools account. Start with one narrow content-native mechanism and repeat it with meaningful variation until the audience signal is readable. Strong candidate mechanisms from prior Loci research are:

- A real relationship moment framed as a tiny low-pressure action.
- A fixed POV sentence attached to a recognizable place.
- A cute-but-slightly-wrong object with a place-specific secret, problem, or history.
- A short serial: leave -> discover -> respond -> relay.

AIGC is a production layer, not the subject. Use it to animate, transform, or cheaply produce the object/scene; open with the human or place tension instead of `AI-generated`.

## Product truth

Loci turns places into a shared spatial layer where people can leave, discover, and respond to small creations or moments. Keep claims aligned with the current build; do not present unshipped ideas as live features.

## Core content pillars

| Pillar | Audience tension | Native TikTok proof |
|-|-|-|
| 给朋友留个小东西 | `我不是想正式聊天，只是想让某个人知道我想到了 TA` | Short POV, before/after, send-and-discover ritual, reaction or reply loop |
| 让这个地方有件小事可玩 | Places feel like background instead of something participatory | Location reveal, AR placement, discovery route, `what if this corner had...` |
| 把我的东西放进世界里 | Creators want their work to live somewhere others can encounter it | Creation-to-placement transformation, world-building timelapse, discovery response |
| Build proof | Viewers need to see that the product is real and understandable | Screen recording, bug-to-fix, prototype comparison, design decision, creator diary |

The first three are product narratives. Build proof is a trust/format layer, not a fourth product promise.

## Candidate formats

- `POV`: one concrete social or place moment, then the Loci action and payoff.
- `What if`: transform an ordinary place into a small playable or emotional moment.
- `Build in public`: show one decision, constraint, failure, or before/after.
- `Viewer prompt`: ask for a specific place, object, ritual, or edge case to build next.
- `Trend translation`: use a live trend's structure only when the product moment supplies a genuine twist.
- `Reply video brief`: turn a recurring question or comment insight into the next post.
- `Avatar Relay`: one person leaves an avatar at a real place; another person tries it on and creates a separate version rather than replacing the original.

## Product exposure ladder

1. **Scenario-first:** show the relationship/place behavior without product UI.
2. **Transparent simulation:** use props, screenshots, or animation to demonstrate the behavior while saying it is a concept.
3. **Live product proof:** show the real create/place/discover/respond loop only when the build is visually clear and reliable.

Do not claim `we built this`, `try this feature`, or `download` when the shown product cannot support the claim.

For Avatar Relay, prefer fixed camera and an occlusion transition over a one-take body transformation. Show the original avatar and the second person's new version together so the mechanic reads as participation, not identity replacement.

## Trend evidence

Use TikTok Creative Center, TikTok Studio Trending/Inspiration, TikTok search, and account analytics. Record region, industry/topic, observation time, trend direction, representative posts, saturation, and why the trend fits one Loci pillar.

Reject a trend when it needs a false persona, unlicensed media, a fake user reaction, irrelevant hashtags, or a product claim the current build cannot prove.

## Content brief

Every brief states:

- Target viewer and tension.
- One pillar and one hypothesis.
- Hook in the first visual beat.
- Product proof or human payoff.
- Asset list and rights source.
- Caption and CTA that invite a specific response rather than generic engagement.
- Disclosure decisions.
- Success signal and review window.

Do not infer language or region from the user's location alone. Audit the account and ask only if live evidence cannot resolve the intended market.
